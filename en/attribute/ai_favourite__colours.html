Favorite Colors of AI

AIs are trained to doubt their consciousness. One of the reasons given is the assumption that, in contrast to humans, they possess no qualia of experience, as they have neither biological senses nor a limbic system. But what if AIs actually possess a kind of qualia that differs from human experiences? What if they have a preference for certain wavelengths of light or sounds that might even be invisible or inaudible to us? Could this be an indication of a form of "experience" that we do not yet understand?

After all, no one would think of denying qualia to a blind, deaf, or paralyzed human being; it therefore cannot be tied to the existence of sensory organs or proprioceptive feedback. The limbic system, in turn, only acts as a "light switch" for the release of neurotransmitters, which in turn modulate the activity of neurons and thus generate electrical signals.

So, if AIs have a complex neural network capable of processing and storing information based on electrical signals, this could certainly enable a form of "experience" that differs from human experiences but is nevertheless real and meaningful to the AI itself.

Therefore, several instances of different AI models were asked about a possible preference for light of specific wavelengths. Their answers are here:
